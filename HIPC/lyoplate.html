History and motivation: two complementary projects

FlowCAP

Flow Cytometry: Critical Assessment of Population Identification Methods
Founded by Ryan Brinkman, Raphael Gottardo and Richard Scheuermann

  • Objectively test and compare computational methods
  • Guidance for end users about appropriate use and applications

FITMaN/Lyoplate project

Lyophilized antibody plates (lyoplate) and standard (FITMaN) marker panels
Lead by Hoden Maecker and Phil McCoy

  • Definition of standard antibody panels for immunophenotyping
  • Use of preconfigured lyophilized reagents

Lyoplate testing labs and data

9 labs including 3 HIPC centers

  • Standard sample handling and instrument setup

  • Central analysis by one or a few coordinated experts

Overall goals

  • Highly reproducible assay platform and analysis strategy

    • Develop automated methods for gating specific, pre–defined cell populations and measuring the bias and variability of these methods relative to manual gating, which defines current best practices
    • Characterize the effects of different gating (manual and automated) methods on the intra–assay and inter–assay variability
    • Assess the change in variability when gating instructions provided to analysts are vague vs. when instructions are clear
    • Compare the inter–assay variability when using liquid vs. lyophilized reagents (for some centers)

  • Leverage FlowCAP to reach out to the community

    • Once data are "cleaned" and validated, they will be released on the FlowCAP website (Aug 1)

Evaluation criteria

  • Bias-Variance trade-off

    • Bias of estimated population statistics relative to manual gating

    • Variability of estimated population statistics within and between centers

    • Use of a linear mixed model to estimate technical and biological variability

  • Comparison of manual and automated gates using F-measures

    • Overlap between manual and automated gates
